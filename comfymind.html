Problem Statement

Many young adults find existing wellness and mental health apps too complicated or intrusive. They need an easy-to-use tool that doesn‚Äôt overwhelm them but still encourages healthy habits. They want an app that respects their privacy, gives them the freedom to set their own pace, and includes additional support like meditation and AI-powered guidance to boost their well-being.

Solution

Comfymind is a simple, privacy-first app designed to help young adults track their moods, complete daily self-improvement tasks, and receive gentle reminders to enhance their mental and physical health. The app has been expanded to include new features like AI support for personalized guidance and a timer for guided meditation with calming music. These features provide users with additional tools to take control of their well-being in a non-intrusive and customizable way.

User Research
Research Goals
  • Identify user needs and expectations for a mood and wellness tracking app.
  • Understand barriers that prevent consistent app usage.
  • Explore how AI and meditation features could enhance user engagement.
Methodology
  • 20 survey responses from users aged 18‚Äì30.
  • 5 in-depth interviews to capture emotional attitudes and usability expectations.
  • Observation sessions to identify friction points in existing wellness apps.
Key Findings
  • Privacy is non-negotiable: Most users are cautious about sharing mental health data and prefer anonymous or local data storage options.
  • Simplicity drives consistency: Participants felt overwhelmed by complex dashboards or mandatory journaling. They preferred quick check-ins using emojis and short tasks.
  • Avoiding pressure improves engagement: Daily streaks and gamification created anxiety. Users valued gentle encouragement over performance tracking.
  • Desire for emotional support: Many users wanted a friendly, conversational AI to provide motivation, empathy, and practical suggestions when feeling low.
  • Mindfulness as a coping tool: Meditation and calming audio were viewed as effective ways to manage stress and improve mood.
